What can AAST do to save energy (smart village )

1. Check How Energy is Used

Look at how much energy the academy uses and find where it’s wasted. Use special tools (like smart electricity meters) to track energy use in real time.

2. Use Better Lighting

Replace regular bulbs with LED bulbs that use less electricity.
Install sensors or timers so lights turn off automatically when no one is in the room.

3. Improve Air Conditioning and Heating

Make sure air conditioners and heaters are cleaned and working well.
Use smart temperature controls to avoid wasting energy when rooms are empty.
Open windows for fresh air instead of using air conditioners when it’s not too hot.

4. Add Solar Panels

Use solar panels on buildings to make electricity from sunlight.
Solar energy can also heat water for bathrooms or kitchens.

5. Use Energy-Saving Devices

Buy machines (like fridges, computers, and printers) that use less power.
Turn off computers and other devices when not in use or set them to sleep mode.

6. Save Water and Energy Together

Install solar-powered water heaters.
Use taps and showers that use less water to save energy needed for pumping or heating water.

7. Teach Everyone About Energy Saving

Explain to students and staff how they can save energy every day (like turning off lights and devices).
Run small events or posters to remind everyone to save energy.

8. Use Green Building Ideas

When building or fixing campus buildings, use materials and designs that keep heat out in summer and in during winter.
Add things like insulation and better windows to reduce electricity for cooling or heating.

9. Encourage Eco-Friendly Travel

Use bicycles or electric cars around the campus.
Set up a carpooling system for students and staff to share rides instead of driving alone.

10. Smart Technology for Energy Control

Use apps or automatic systems to control lights, air conditioners, and other devices.
Let technology help predict when energy is needed and when it isn’t.
